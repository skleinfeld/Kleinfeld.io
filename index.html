Biography

Saul B. Kleinfeld was born and raised in El Paso, Texas, named in honor of his grandfather, Saul D. Kleinfeld, a civic leader and champion of amateur sports in the community. His grandfather passed away just three days before he was born, leaving behind a legacy of service, leadership, and love for sport that continues to inspire Saul today.

From an early age, Saul immersed himself in baseball, first playing in Frank Manning Little League and carrying his Yankee pride from birth. Over the years, he has continued to play competitively and now suits up for the East Dallas Expos in the Veterans Division while also managing and playing with Gann’s Bulls in the Rookie Division. Baseball, for Saul, is more than a pastime — it is a lifelong teacher of resilience, teamwork, and growth.

Saul is a Senior Product Manager at RazorMetrics, where he develops creative technology solutions to complex challenges. Among his proudest accomplishments is leading the design of a new internal configuration tool to streamline the onboarding of new customers, demonstrating his ability to blend vision with execution. His leadership philosophy is rooted in servant leadership, always putting his team first and empowering others to succeed.

Earlier in his career, Saul drove major initiatives at Aveanna Healthcare, shaping operational processes and systems during a period of significant organizational change. These experiences sharpened his ability to build scalable frameworks that bring clarity to complex environments.

Outside of work, Saul is an Eagle Scout with more than 25 fifty-mile backpacking and canoeing treks completed in destinations ranging from Alaska and Hawaii to Colorado and New Zealand — where his Boy Scout troop once made international news after being stranded near Lake Coleridge. He also has a passion for cooking, traveling, and creating memorable experiences with his wife, Hannah, along with their two dogs and four cats.

For Saul, life mirrors the lessons of baseball. You win some and you lose some, but what matters most is staying in the game. He carries that perspective into everything he does — as a product leader, a league executive, a teammate, and a builder of systems that outlast him.
